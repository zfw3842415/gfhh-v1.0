Big Ideas that Make Brands Matter

“We Sell or Else”

 

When David Ogilvy founded Ogilvy & Mather in 1948 in New York City, his straightforward principle surrounding advertising would remain at the core of what we do, following us as we moved across markets and around the globe.

 

While our belief in branding and the power of brands hasn’t changed, we do see ourselves as a brand agency for the digital world. Today, brands are facing a more and more fragmented world, where consumers have infinite choices and information at their fingertips. We believe that strong ideas and creativity are needed, now more than ever, in order for a brand to connect with its’ consumers. 

 

Our ideas are driven by deep understanding of China’s consumers, media and digital landscape. We develop strong ideas that transcend media, and are brought to life masterfully connected across every relevant touchpoint.

 

As David Ogilvy said: “Unless your advertising contains a big idea, it will pass like a ship in the night.”

Graham Fink

Chief Creative Officer
Ogilvy & Mather China

Graham Fink is one of the most highly awarded creative directors in the world. After one year with O&M China, Graham led the agency to win O&M Asia’s first-ever Grand Prix at the prestigious Cannes Lions for Coca-Cola’s “Coke Hands” in 2012. During his career he has created some of advertising’s most memorable campaigns including the iconic British Airways commercial, “Face,” recognized as one of the 100 greatest ads of all time. He and his teams have won countless awards for his work at Cannes Lions, D&AD Pencils, One Show, LIAA, EPICA, EUROBEST, British Television Arrows, Campaign Press and Poster Awards and four BAFTA’s.
