T.B. Song

Chairman, Ogilvy & Mather Greater China Chairman, WPP Greater China

With more than 40 years of experience in the communications industry, 30 of which have been with O&M and WPP, T.B. Song is arguably one of the most experienced ad men in China. T.B. began his career in Taiwan in the 1970s. In 1991 he was named Chairman of O&M China, with the responsibility of developing the mainland China market, starting with Shanghai. In 1998 he became a member of the O&M Worldwide Board. T.B. is often recognized for his vast contributions to the Chinese ad industry. He became Chairman of WPP Greater China in 2006 and was named among Campaign’s “Global Power List 2010.”

Shenan Chuang

Vice Chairman, Ogilvy & Mather Greater China

In 2014, Shenan Chuang was promoted to Vice Chairman of O&M Greater China after 7 successful years as the company’s CEO. She is one of the most respected and influential people in the Chinese advertising world, and the first Chinese woman to join the O&M Worldwide Board. Shenan joined O&M in 1985 in Taipei and has been with the agency ever since, relocating to Beijing in 2003. She has dedicated her career and passion to helping Chinese and multinational clients build powerful brands. She is known for the unique, dynamic corporate culture she has created and is also know for her appreciation for creativity and communication, shared with her more than 5 million followers on Sina Weibo. In 2014, CNN featured Shenan as a “Leading Women” and in 2013 she was honored among Forbes Asia’s “Women in the Mix.” Shenan is also the co-founder of China 4A and served as the organization’s Chairman in 2006, 2007 and 2010.

Chris Reitermann

Co-CEO, Ogilvy & Mather Asia Pacific

President, Ogilvy & Mather Advertising, Asia Pacific

Chris has spent more than 19 years with O&M Group, 15 of those in China, and he has garnered a wealth of experience across a range of disciplines, clients and geographies. He is a deep believer in the power of brands, whether they be multinational companies in the Chinese market, or home-grown brands looking to “go out” across the world. He is also a pioneer in digital innovation, and is one of the key figures helping companies transform the way they think about reaching new consumers.

Graham Fink

Chief Creative Officer, Ogilvy & Mather China

Graham Fink is one of the most highly awarded creative directors in the world. After just one year with O&M China, Graham led the agency to win O&M Asia’s first-ever Grand Prix at the prestigious Cannes Lions for Coca-Cola’s “Coke Hands” work in 2012. During his career he has created some of advertising’s most memorable campaigns including the iconic British Airways commercial, “Face,” recognized as one of the 100 greatest ads of all time. Having won countless awards for his work at Cannes Lions, D&AD Pencils, One Show, LIAA, EPICA, EUROBEST, British Television Arrows, Campaign Press and Poster Awards and four BAFTA’s, Graham was the youngest ever president of D&AD (Design & Art Directors Association) in 1996. Graham is also a prolific commercial and music video director, filmmaker, artist and photographer.

Scott Kronick

President and CEO, Ogilvy Public Relations, Asia Pacific

Scott Kronick joined Ogilvy Public Relations in New York in 1987. Today his public relations expertise spans both the U.S. and Asia Pacific region. Scott was appointed Regional President and CEO of Ogilvy Public Relations in 2014, after having served for four years as the President of Ogilvy PR, North Asia.
As one of the two founding members of Ogilvy PR in China, Scott plays an instrumental role in the leadership and management of the China operation. Today, Ogilvy PR is the one of the largest and most influential public relations firms in China. In addition to his many accomplishments, Scott has been the recipient of the Individual Achievement SABRE Award in 2012, China International PR Association’s Lifetime Achievement Award in 2010, and was named “PR Agency Head of the Year” in 2008 at the Asia Pacific PR Awards. Under his leadership, Ogilvy PR was named “Asia Pacific Consultancy of the Year” by The Holmes Report in 2009, “PR Consultancy of the Year” at the Asia Pacific PR Awards in 2008 and “Network of the Year in Asia Pacific” by PRWeek in 2002.

Debby Cheung

President, Ogilvy & Mather Shanghai

Debby’s expertise lies in corporate and consumer communications, as well as crisis management. Over the years, she has advised clients across a range of industries including luxury, food, tourism and retail. She has deep insight in company strategy and brand building. She joined Ogilvy PR, Hong Kong in 1988 as an associate and rose to Managing Director in 1998. After a two-year stint at the Mission Hills Group in 2001 as their Executive Director overseeing corporate affairs, sales and marketing, she rejoined O&M Shanghai in 2003. In 2015, she was recognized with the Outstanding Achievement Award by SABRE

Jacco ter Schegget

President, OgilvyOne China

Jacco has more than 16 years’ experience in digital, marketing and communications. The OgilvyOne team he leads focuses on digital and customer engagement, and he is the driver of its the strategic direction, growth and development in China, which includes leading client opportunities, new business efforts, staff development and creating new products and services. His move east follows a wildly successful seven years at DDB & Tribal, during which he was European President of Tribal Worldwide, DDB’s digital arm, co-CEO of DDB & Tribal Amsterdam and Digital Officer for the DDB Group in Europe. Under his leadership, DDB & Tribal Amsterdam was named “Digital Agency of the Year” by Dutch Industry Prize for Digital Media and became one of the most awarded European digital agencies and the first digital agency to win a Cannes Film Grand Prix in 2009.

Stephen Wai

CFO, Ogilvy & Mather Greater China

Joining Ogilvy in 2005, Stephen Wai believes that continuous innovation, thinking “outside-the-box” and keeping up good team spirit are key success factors for a company. Prior to joining Ogilvy, Stephen spent 5 years with PCCW, where – in addition to financial experience – he gained a depth of knowledge in sales and marketing strategies. Stephen also accrued experience on various aspects of financial knowledge with PricewaterhouseCoopers, Goldman Sachs and a private investment company.

Michael Tam

President, H&O China
CEO, Ogilvy Fashion China

Michael Tam is known industry-wide as a champion of change and hails from a diverse professional background as a 16-year brand marketer, governmental and non-governmental consultant, and art gallery founder and curator. He is an expert in the role social and content marketing plays in the wider world and how brands and people must communicate in order to be heard in the Chinese market. He oversees H&O China, a joint venture between Hogarth and Redworks, which realizes creative ideas using in-house teams working across all media: complex CGI, high end post production, fast turnaround online content, global integrated campaigns or simple local production.

Tracy Fu

CEO, Geometry Global China

Tracy Fu leads the China operation of Geometry Global, the world’s largest activation network for the digital age born out of the merger between G2, OgilvyAction and JWTAction in 2013. Tracy’s journey started in 1997, when she co-founded Dawson Integrated Marketing Company and pioneered the first generation of below the line activation tools and talent in China. In 2007, Dawson joined the WPP Group and Tracy led the Dawson Group as CEO. Dawson grew in size, reputation, capabilities and clients under Tracy’s leadership, earning recognition from clients for being an “Outstanding Supplier” in 2013 and 2014. She is now the CEO of Geometry Global, China, the most reputable activation agency in the country.

Mickey Chak

Chief Planning Officer, Ogilvy & Mather China

Mickey leads a strong team of planners that work to uncover and decipher consumer insights across Asia Pacific markets. Mickey’s wide roster of clients spans categories and geographies and includes renowned international and domestic Chinese brands. He is an active speaker and lecturer on brand building and communications in mainland China and Hong Kong and also contributes greatly to O&M’s thought leadership.

Michael Zhang

CEO, Neo@Ogilvy China

Michael Zhang is a 20 year media veteran. He first started his career in Ogilvy & Mather in Beijing. Over past 2 decades, Michael has held several senior management positions in GroupM, including President of Mediacom China and Chief Strategy Officer of GroupM China. He currently oversees the Neo@Ogilvy China business across Beijing, Shanghai, and Guangzhou, working to drive an aggressive growth strategy and develop new services in Performance Media.

Selina Teng

President, Ogilvy & Mather Beijing

A native of Beijing, Selina joined Ogilvy Public Relations in 1999 and has been with Ogilvy & Mather ever since. Over the years, she has grown the IBM team into a sustainable technology practice for Ogilvy PR, pushing and developing it to keep Ogilvy ahead of the digital curve. Selina also helped build a highly successful, specialized automotive team, providing services to top international and local automotive brands since 2008. In addition to her role with Ogilvy PR, she also oversees the day-to-day business of the Ogilvy & Mather Group in Beijing with all Beijing units reporting to her.
